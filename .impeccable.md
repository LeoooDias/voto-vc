## Design Context

### Users
Brazilian voters preparing for the 2026 federal elections. They range from politically engaged citizens to first-time voters curious about who represents their values. They arrive wanting clarity — "who votes like I would?" — and leave with actionable insight. Context: often on mobile, likely skeptical of political tools, need to trust the data immediately.

### Brand Personality
**Accessible, civic, neutral.** The brand speaks with institutional credibility but without institutional coldness. It respects the user's intelligence without overwhelming them. It is deliberately non-partisan — the design must never suggest political leaning through color, imagery, or tone.

### Emotional Goal
**Empowerment.** The primary feeling should be: "Now I know who represents me." Users should feel informed, capable, and ready to make better voting decisions. The interface should make complex legislative data feel approachable and personal.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Bold minimalism — sharp, typographically driven, generous whitespace, zero border-radius. Every element earns its place.
- **References**: The Pudding, FiveThirtyEight — data journalism that is visually sophisticated, interactive, and treats the reader as intelligent. Clean data visualization with editorial confidence.
- **Anti-references**: Generic government portals (gov.br), cluttered news sites, gamified political quizzes. Nothing that feels cheap, partisan, or patronizing.
- **Theme**: Light and dark modes with 6 user-selectable color themes. Default is warm amber on beige (light) / amber on dark navy (dark).

### Design Principles

1. **Typography is the interface.** Weight, size, and spacing do the heavy lifting — not color, not decoration. Headlines at 900 weight, body at 400. Extreme scale contrast (4.5rem h1 vs 0.688rem labels). Letter-spacing ranges from -0.05em (headlines) to +0.25em (uppercase labels).

2. **Whitespace communicates confidence.** Generous padding and margins signal that every element matters. Don't fill space — let it breathe. Hero sections use 8rem+ top padding. Sections separated by space, not borders.

3. **Sharp geometry, zero softness.** Border-radius: 0 everywhere — cards, buttons, modals, inputs, toasts, photos. This creates a distinctive editorial feel that separates voto.vc from generic rounded-corner UI. The only exception is semantic elements (vote color dots, progress circles).

4. **Color serves data, not decoration.** The red-to-green voting spectrum is sacred and semantic. Accent colors are theme-customizable but always secondary to the content. Background colors are muted (beige/dark navy); text and borders do the structural work.

5. **Neutrality is a design choice.** No visual element should suggest political alignment. Avoid imagery of politicians, party logos, or flag colors in non-thematic contexts. The Canarinho theme is an opt-in celebration of Brazilian identity, not a default.

### Accessibility Requirements
- **Target**: WCAG AAA where feasible, minimum WCAG AA everywhere
- **Existing**: High-contrast theme, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored, focus-visible styling, ARIA labels on all interactive elements, keyboard navigation, 44px minimum touch targets
- **Typography**: Plus Jakarta Sans at all weights (400-900), antialiased rendering, adequate line-heights (1.0 headings, 1.6+ body)
- **Color**: Semantic vote colors (red/green) always paired with text labels, never color-only

### Technical Constraints
- **CSS**: Scoped Svelte `<style>` blocks with CSS custom properties (no Tailwind, no CSS-in-JS library)
- **Theming**: Dual-layer system — `data-theme` (claro/escuro) + `data-color-theme` (6 options) on root element
- **Fonts**: Google Fonts (Plus Jakarta Sans), loaded via `<link>` in app.html
- **Responsive**: Mobile-first, breakpoints at 480px, 600px, 768px
- **Framework**: SvelteKit 5, Svelte 5 runes syntax
